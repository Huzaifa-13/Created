The Four Learned Brahmins

Once upon a time there lived four friends, ram jaganath, bhola and chetan.
They were all learned. One day, they were passing through a forest."There
are wild animals here, let's be careful ," said bhola. While they were deep
in the forest, they came upon the remains of a lion. "Aha! What is this?
the skeleton of a lion! I can use a mantra and put these bones together,"
boasted Chetan. As the three friends watched, he closed his eyes and chanted
a prayer.The next moment, the bones of the lion rose high up and started to
float in the air. Slowly, they joined together one by one to form a perfect
skeleton."Here you are," said Chetan, proudly."Hah, that is noting. I can give
this skeleton skin and flesh," said Ram with a smirk. Now ram closed his eyes
and started to chant a mantra. gradually, flesh started to grow on the bones
of the lion. Then the flesh started to get covered with skin. until the entire
lion looked almost life-likie. "There, see! I can do better than you," said Ram
looking at Chetan. Then Jaganath spoke. He said, "You two have done well. But
with my superior learnings, I can give this lion life." There aws a hushed
silence. Then bhola said, "No, don't do that. The lion is sure to eat alll of
us. It is a fierce beast after all." Jaganath said angrily, "You are just
jealous, Bhola." Chetan and Ram too shouted at Bhola. "If you cannot do anything,
don't stop us," they said. "Alright! Do as you please. But I am going to a safe
place. I will climb up that tree," said Bhola and climed at the top of the tree.
Let him run! The coward! said Jaganath. He closed his eyes and started chanting
loudly. Jaganath kept chanting the mantra for a long time. Suddenly there was a
loud roar. "Rrrrrooar!" Jaganath opend his eyes, only to see the lion pounce on
him."Oh my God! What have I done!" screamed Jaganath, but it was too late. In
no time, the lion had killed the three friends. After a little while, the
lion left. Bhola got off the tree. "My poor friends! They would be alive now
if they knew how to put their knowledge to use," he tought to himself. Bhola
then went to a nearby town and put his learning to good use. In time, he became
succesful and wise.

The Clever Monkey and the Crocodile

On a mango tree by the river, lived a monkey. The tree was always filled with
the sweetest mangoes in the forest. The monkey was happy as he got enough to
eat from the tree. In the river, there liveda crocodile. Whenever the crocodile came
to the banks of river, the monkey would throw him a mango. As time [passed, the
crocodile and the monkey became friends. The crocodile would come everyday to
meet the monkey and eat the mangoes. One day the crocodile took a mongo to his
wife. When the crocodile's wife ate the mango, she said, "I've never tasted
anything so good in my whole life. You must bring me the monkey. Can you imagine
how sweet the monkey's heart would be! He eats these mangoes everyday." "The
monkey is my friend," pleaded the crocodile, but his wife would'nt listen. Finally,
the crocodile went to the monkey with a plan. He said, "My wife invites you to
dinner". "I can't swim," said the monkey, "Oh, that is no problem. You can sit on my
back," said the crocodile. So the monkey and the crocodile started their journey
across the river. Half way to the crocodile's home, the crocodile said, "I am sorry,
friend, but my wife wants to eat your heart. That's why I am taking you with me.
The monkey was shoked when he heard this. He thought, "What shall I do?"
